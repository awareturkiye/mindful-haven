What is Mental Health?

Understanding the foundations of mental wellbeing — and why it matters for all of us.

The Basics

Mental Health is About How We Think, Feel & Act

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social wellbeing. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices.

Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood. It is not simply the absence of mental illness — it is a state of positive wellbeing in which you can realise your potential, cope with the normal stresses of life, work productively, and contribute to your community.

💡 Key fact: 1 in 4 people in the UK will experience a mental health problem each year. Yet many people suffer in silence. You are not alone, and help is always available.

🧠
Why It Matters

Mental and Physical Health are Deeply Linked

Poor mental health affects every part of your life — and your body. Taking care of your mind is not a luxury; it is a necessity.

❤️

Physical Health

Poor mental health increases the risk of chronic physical health conditions, including heart disease, diabetes, and weakened immunity.

🤝

Relationships

Our mental state shapes how we connect with family, friends, and colleagues. Good mental health builds strong, meaningful relationships.

💼

Work & Productivity

Mental health problems are one of the leading causes of workplace absence. Proper support leads to better focus, creativity, and performance.

Awareness

Signs of Good Mental Health

✓

Emotional Resilience

Ability to bounce back from setbacks and adapt to change without being overwhelmed.

✓

Healthy Relationships

Forming and maintaining positive, supportive connections with others.

✓

Sense of Purpose

Feeling that life has meaning and direction, even during difficult times.

✓

Managing Emotions

Recognising and expressing emotions in healthy, constructive ways.

Warning Signs

Signs You May Need Support

!

Persistent Low Mood

Feeling sad, empty, or hopeless for more than two weeks without a clear reason.

!

Excessive Worry

Being unable to control worry that interferes with daily life and relationships.

!

Social Withdrawal

Pulling away from friends, family, and activities you used to enjoy.

!

Significant Changes in Sleep or Appetite

Sleeping too much or too little; eating significantly more or less than usual.

Self-Care

How to Look After Your Mental Health

Small, consistent actions can make a meaningful difference. You don't have to change everything at once.

🚶

Move Your Body

Even a 20-minute walk releases endorphins that improve mood and reduce anxiety.

💬

Talk About It

Sharing your feelings with someone you trust can relieve pressure and offer new perspective.

🍃

Connect with Nature

Spending time outdoors lowers cortisol levels and provides a natural calming effect.

🎯

Set Boundaries

Learning to say no and protecting your energy is an act of self-respect, not selfishness.

😴

Prioritise Sleep

Aim for 7–9 hours of consistent, quality sleep each night for better mood regulation.

🌟

Celebrate Small Wins

Acknowledge your progress, however small. Every step forward is worth recognising.
