Get Help Now

Whether you're in crisis or just starting to struggle — support is available. You deserve help, and it's okay to ask for it.

Self-Help

Steps You Can Take Right Now

You don't have to wait for professional support to start feeling better. These steps can make a real difference today.

1

Acknowledge how you're feeling

Name your emotions without judgement. Saying "I feel anxious" or "I'm struggling" is the first powerful act of self-care. You don't have to be fine.

2

Tell someone you trust

Talking to a friend, family member, or colleague can relieve the weight you're carrying. You don't need to have all the answers — just share what you can.

3

Try a grounding technique

The 5-4-3-2-1 technique: name 5 things you can see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste. This anchors you to the present moment.

4

Practise box breathing

Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 4, hold for 4. Repeat 4–6 times. This activates your parasympathetic nervous system and reduces acute anxiety within minutes.

5

Limit news and social media

Constant exposure to distressing content amplifies anxiety. Give yourself permission to step away. Your wellbeing matters more than staying informed 24/7.

6

See your GP

If things persist or worsen, make an appointment with your GP. They can assess your needs, provide referrals to talking therapies, and discuss medication options if appropriate.

Professional Support

Finding the Right Professional Help

There are many types of professional mental health support. Finding what works for you is part of the process.

🏥

Your GP

Your first port of call. Your GP can assess your mental health, prescribe medication, and refer you to talking therapies through the NHS. You can tell your GP anything in confidence.

NHS Services →
🗣️

Talking Therapy

CBT, counselling, psychotherapy, and more. Available through the NHS (IAPT) or privately. You can also self-refer to IAPT services in England at nhs.uk.

Learn More →
💊

Medication

Antidepressants, mood stabilisers, and other medications can help manage mental health conditions, often alongside therapy. Always discuss options with a qualified medical professional.

Ask a Question →
🌐

Online Therapy

Accessing therapy online can remove barriers around travel, stigma, or waiting lists. Services like BetterHelp, Talkspace, or NHS-approved apps offer accessible alternatives.

See Resources →
👥

Support Groups

Connecting with others who understand your experience can be incredibly healing. Local groups, online communities, and peer support services all provide this connection.

Find Groups →
📱

Mental Health Apps

Apps like Headspace, Calm, Wysa, and Woebot offer guided support between therapy sessions. Best used as a supplement to, not replacement for, professional care.

Practical Advice

What to Say When You Ask for Help

Many people struggle to find the words. Here are some phrases that can help you start the conversation:

"I've been feeling really low/anxious/overwhelmed and I'm not sure how to cope. I think I need some support."

"I wanted to talk to you because I trust you. I've been struggling lately and I didn't want to keep it to myself."

"I'm not in crisis, but I'm not okay either. Can we talk?"

Remember: you do not need to have all the words. Starting the conversation, however imperfectly, is what matters most.
